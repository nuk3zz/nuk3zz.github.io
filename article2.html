Android 14 Beta: What’s New and How to Install It

Isuru Sampath
31 July

As Android 14 inches closer to its official release, the beta version has already provided us with a glimpse of what to expect. Packed with new features and improvements, Android 14 is shaping up to be a significant update for Android users. If you're eager to try out the latest features before everyone else, the beta version is available for installation on select devices.

1. Improved Privacy and Security

OAndroid 14 continues Google’s focus on privacy and security. The beta introduces new app permission features, including more granular control over which apps can access your data. Additionally, apps that don’t need full access to your photos and videos can be given permission to access only specific files, enhancing your control over personal data.

2. Customizable Lock Screen

One of the most noticeable changes in Android 14 is the ability to customize your lock screen. You can now personalize it with widgets, change the clock style, and even add shortcuts to frequently used apps. This feature allows users to tailor their lock screen to better suit their needs and aesthetic preferences.

3. Battery Efficiency Enhancements

Battery life has always been a critical concern for smartphone users, and Android 14 addresses this with various under-the-hood optimizations. The beta version includes better power management features that extend battery life by reducing background app activity and improving the efficiency of system processes.

4. Per-App Language Preferences

In Android 14, you can set different language preferences for individual apps. This is particularly useful for multilingual users who prefer to use different languages in different contexts. The feature is easy to access through the system settings, making it convenient to switch languages for specific apps.

5. Advanced Accessibility Features

Android 14 introduces several new accessibility features aimed at making the OS more user-friendly for people with disabilities. This includes a new magnifier tool that can zoom in on any part of the screen, improved text-to-speech functionality, and enhanced visual settings to help users with low vision.
